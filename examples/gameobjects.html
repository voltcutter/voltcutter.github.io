Gameobjects

What are they.

They are really important because they are game objects. They can have all sorts behaviours depending on what components you give them. Components are just behaviours for the Gameobject.

For example, if you wanted a Gameobject to be a bright light, you could give a Light component to it. Or if you wanted a Gameobject to have physics, look like a flashlight, and give off light, you can give it Rigidbody, Mesh Collider, Mesh Filter, Mesh Renderer, and Light component.

Components

A good way to learn is to follow examples. This example will tell you how to make a cube with physics.

1. Create a cube. You can do this from going to the Gameobject tab at the very top of the Unity Editor window. Then go to 3D, and click Cube.

2. Make sure the cube has a collider or else it will just fall through stuff into the abyss.

3. If the cube doesn't have a collider, click the "Add Component" button in the inspector. Then search up "Box Collider" and click on it.

4. Add a Rigidbody to add physics to this cube. Click on "Add Component" in the inspector and search up "Rigidbody". And add it.

5. Currently, there is nothing to collide with so it will just be the camera, cube, light, and nothing else. So it will just fall into the abyss.

6. Create a plane by going to Gameobject/3D/Plane tab at the top of the Unity Editor window.

7. Make sure to position the plane below the cube by moving it.
